Career Skills Challenge- Web Page Design & Construction

Welcome to the New York City Website Competition!

On behalf of the Web Design program at Coalinga High School, we thank you for participating in this event. Please realize that you probably will not have enough time to complete the project. The judges take that into consideration when reviewing your work.

You are charged with making a promotional website for the City of New York. The focus of your project will be tourism in New York City and its five major boroughs, the Bronx, Brooklyn, Manhattan, Queens, and Staten Island. Sell the concept of a tourist friendly New York City while showcasing the beauty of the city.

Requirements:

Create a website of six linked web pages detailing the various buroughs of New York city. Use an appropriate color scheme and build pages for an audience whose age ranges from 25-40 years old. You can gather facts about the the buroughs of New York City from the supplied Word documents.

Use CSS and HTML to build the website. You may embed CSS in the pages or use a linked global style sheet.

The client has provided you a series of photos and text (Word documents). You are limited to using only these items, or your project will be disqualified.

You are to incorporate a minimum of ten images from those provided. You are free to crop and/or consolidate images using Photoshop. You must create your own logo for the website.

Setup:

Download the provided zip file onto your Desktop. Extract the zip file. This will create a CSCNYCProject folder. Inside this folder, you have been provided a Resources folder with the needed text, image, and video files. Make sure to save images you use on your website within your Images folder. Do not link to images in the Resources folder. Save the video inside the outer Video folder. Incoporate the video on your landing page.

Write a one paragraph introduction about the city and its five buroughs on the landing page. You can use the provided NewYork.docx Word file as your source of information.

Here are additional requirements:

  • Name the landing page of your site index.html
  • Include an appropriate title on each page
  • Name each page with an obvious and logical file name
  • Leave the site displayed on your computer screen when finished and call over a judge.

If you have any concerns, please raise your hand and a teacher assistant will help you.

The catch:

The images are not appropriately sized for Internet presentation. You will have to adjust the file size and resolution.

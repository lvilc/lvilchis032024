Macbeth Scavenger Hunt

Reflection

In the spirit of easter, I thought a Macbeth Scavenger Hunt would be a fun idea. There are so many quotes from this play that are just so interesting and I thought it'd be fun to try and jog people's memory to find which scene it belongs to. Below, is a reflection generally why I chose all of the quotes I did.


Macbeth

The quotes that I chose, show the progression of Macbeth's descent into madness. He went from questioning whether or not the witches' prophecy could be good or bad, to being paranoid about becoming a murderer, to believing that only the witches' prophecy is correct and that he is a supreme being. He became egotistical to an extreme.

Lady Macbeth

Much like Macbeth's descent into madness, Lady Macbeth had her craze into the opposite direction, where she became more paranoid from her overconfidence going into the situation. Connecting both Macbeth and Lady Macbeth's descent into madness, they had a complete swap of mentalities following the murder of King Duncan.

Banquo

Banquo is a character we hardly see because he passes away in the middle of the story. However, he is a character who has; the best way to describe it; spidey senses. He has gut feelings as to what is going on, but he never truly learns until his death, in which his suspicions were correct. He is a tragic character, as he was betrayed and assassinated by his friend.

Macduff

Macduff is a powerful character who is always in the center of many of the conflicts in the story, and the quotes highlight that. He is the one who discovered King Duncan's body, had his wife and son murdered, and was the one who ultimately murdered Macbeth. It isn't until the apparitions appear to give Macbeth their prophecies do we realize just how powerful of a character he is and how he, is technically the villain, and the hero of the story.

Three Witches

The witches are the ones who created this domino effect which is the story of Macbeth. As said before, for a new reader, the witches are sketchy, but it doesn't seem to be too serious. Each of the quotes are the beginning of a new trouble in the story and it isn't until much later that it is realized. Each line is a chant which all fall into place, becoming more and more grave.

Extras

The porter: That quote is a important though it seems like just a funny scene as later on, Macbeth becomes much like the devil as he becomes a tyrant and even dabbled further into the witches' magic.
The apparition: The beginning of Macbeth, he was severely paranoid of the consequences of his actions, however, following King Duncan's death, he becomes bolder and yet crazier with his hallucinations. This quote, was the cause of Macbeth becoming overly confident of himself.
The messenger: While it isn't so important of a quote to remember, it was the real push for Macduff's revenge against Macbeth. It also depicts just how deep Macbeth's cruelty really is, as he sent assassins to murder a political opponent's wife and child.
